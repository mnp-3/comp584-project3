1.The Book Club for Troublesome Women

4.5's

“If women supported each other the way men do, the world would look very different.” The Book Club for Troublesome Women is a beautiful, moving novel that left a lasting impression on me. Set in the 1960s, it captures the strength and resilience of sisterhood in a way that still resonates today. I’ll absolutely be recommending this to friends, and I urge anyone reading this to give it a chance. It’s inspiring in all the right ways, and I can’t wait to dive into more of Bostwick’s work. It also gave me the same vibe as the show Desperate Housewives, which I absolutely love! So if you're also a fan you'll love this one!
